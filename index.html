About Asha Physiotherapy

Asha Physiotherapy was established in Mahendranagar, Kanchanpur, to meet the growing demand for quality rehabilitation services in the far-western region of Nepal. For years, patients needing post-surgery care, injury rehabilitation, or pain management had to travel to cities like Kathmandu, as there were no qualified specialists locally. Since opening our doors over three years ago, we have been serving 300+ patients every month, providing expert and compassionate care right here in the community. Our team uses modern techniques and evidence-based practices to help patients regain mobility, recover from injuries, and improve their quality of life without the need for long-distance travel.

About the Founder

Suraj Lekhak, the founder of Asha Physiotherapy, is a highly qualified physiotherapist with both a Bachelor's and Master's degree in Physiotherapy from M.V. Shetty College, Mangalore, India. He began his career working at a prestigious hospital in Mangalore, where he gained invaluable experience in a well-equipped, fast-paced clinical environment. In 2020, during the COVID-19 pandemic, Suraj returned to Nepal. The absence of physiotherapy clinics in his hometown of Mahendranagar, coupled with the opportunity to be closer to his family, inspired him to establish Asha Physiotherapy, the first dedicated clinic of its kind in the region. Today, Suraj's expertise is so sought after that, in addition to running the clinic, he also provides consulting hours at Mahakali Zonal Hospital and Mantra Hospital, Mahendranagar.

Our Services

We offer a wide range of therapies to treat various conditions.

Post-Surgery Rehabilitation

  • Personalized recovery plans tailored to surgery type
  • Restore strength and mobility
  • Focus on pain reduction and functional independence

Injury Rehabilitation

  • Treat sports injuries, fractures, sprains
  • Prevent future injuries through strengthening exercises
  • Provide guidance on safe return-to-activity protocols

Pain Management

  • Manage arthritis, back and neck pain
  • Use evidence-based therapies including manual therapy and electrotherapy
  • Educate patients on lifestyle changes to reduce pain

Neurological Rehabilitation

  • Support stroke and nerve injury recovery
  • Improve movement, coordination, and daily living skills
  • Use specialized techniques like proprioceptive neuromuscular facilitation

Pediatric Physiotherapy

  • Assist children with developmental delays
  • Gentle and effective treatments adapted to each child's needs
  • Engage children with play-based therapy techniques

Geriatric Physiotherapy

  • Maintain mobility and balance
  • Improve independence and quality of life
  • Fall prevention strategies and strength training

Workplace & Ergonomic Therapy

  • Correct posture and reduce strain
  • Exercises for desk workers and repetitive tasks
  • Provide ergonomic assessments and personalized workplace advice
